Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1970: Diego joined Los Cebollitas, the children's division of Argentinos Juniors, where the legend began.
  • 1976: Diego played his first game as a professional.
  • 1981: Debut in Boca
  • 1984: Presentation in Naples.After having played in Barcelona -where he did not have a very successful pass- and after a first contact with the European soccer, the Ten was presented as a new player of Napoli before 80.000 fans who filled the San Paolo Stadium in his presentation.
  • 1986: Two legendary goals against England.
  • 1986: Diego and the World Cup , the only one that Diego won, the second in the history of the Argentine National Team. Maradona played a huge championship, and took our country to the consecration in Mexico. He was the outstanding figure of the tournament, with 5 goals, 4 assists and a performance that marked him as the King of World Football.
  • 1990: Ankle to misery and runner-up in Italy. Maradona played from the third match (vs. Romania) of the World Cup in Italy with a broken ankle, given the kicks he suffered. He must have infiltrated to play the rest of the competition, although he did not stop shining.
  • 1994: Diego's last goal with Argentina. "El grito", was Diego's unbridled relief in the foreground of a camera, after converting to Greece in the World Cup in the United States.
  • 1994: Positive doping and out of the World Cup. "They cut off my legs", that's how Maradona himself described the hardest blow of his career after the match against Nigeria. After the 2-1 victory against the Africans, Diego went through the anti-doping and was found to have consumed ephedrine.
  • 1994: Debut as a coach.The sanction for doping in the U.S. left him off the courts for fifteen months, but far from sitting still, the Ten decided to start his career as a DT
  • 1996: Return to soccer and Boca. Having also managed Racing, Maradona returned to soccer after the suspension. It was with 36 years and returning to the club of his loves: Boca. A year later, after many ups and downs and without having won any titles, Diego announced his retirement from soccer after a Superclásico against River.
  • 2001: Farewell party at La Bombonera. Before a stadium filled with 60,000 people, Diego had his tribute match in La Boca, with luxury guests.
  • 2005: More health problems.Diego was on the verge of death: he suffered a cardiorespiratory crisis as a result of a hypertension condition, which almost cost him his life.
  • 2005: Debut as a TV host. After recovering from the post-operative process, Diego recovered his silhouette and was encouraged to make his first TV strip. It was on La Noche del Diez, a program that had 13 episodes and featured luxury guests: Pelé, Fidel Castro, Lionel Messi, Zinedine Zidane and Roberto Gómez Bolaños, among others. His first game was a friendly against Scotland with a 1-0 win as an away player, and for the points on March 28, 2009, in a 4-0 win against Venezuela for the 11th date of the South Africa 2010 qualifiers.
  • 2019: Resurgence and return to Argentine soccer. After passing through several clubs in the Middle East and Dorados de Sinaloa in Mexico, Gimnasia de La Plata hired him to save the team from relegation and was a resurgence for Diego. Maradona returned to soccer in our country, he felt again the popular clamor and to be himself.
